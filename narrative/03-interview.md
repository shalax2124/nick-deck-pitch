# Interview Day — Questions for Nick

> Source: [Interview Questions Notion](https://www.notion.so/carenbloom/Interview-Questions-for-Nick-Kyrgios-350af6a343728000be67fcb6971ba2dc) — Crystal Lo

15 questions, three escalating parts. Designed as springboards, not scripts — Nick's strongest moments are unscripted.

## Production rules
- Part 1 should feel like a podcast — loose, fun, no pressure
- Part 2 is the bridge — still conversational but with purpose
- Part 3 is performance — Nick in character, channeling the storyboard energy
- **Let him riff.** The cuts will come from where he goes off-script.

---

## Part 1 — Hobbies & Personal (warm-up)

1. **Kevin Garnett**: spirit animal energy. If KG walked in right now, what's the first thing you'd say?
   *Intent: instant animation. Basketball + KG = Nick's happy place.*
2. **Pokémon tattoo**: full Pokédex on his back. If you could only keep one tattoo for the rest of your life, which one survives?
   *Intent: opinionated micro-stakes. Possible visual moment — he might turn around and point.*
3. **Sushi**: brought sushi to a Wimbledon presser. Walk us through your perfect order — no budget, no rules.
   *Intent: he's called sushi "romantic" and "an event." Genuine relaxed answer.*
4. **Gaming**: claims to be the best on the ATP tour. Who's #2, and how badly do you destroy them?
   *Intent: trash-talk is his love language.*
5. **Cars**: Demon, Lambo, GTR. Trade them all for one car you'd drive forever — what is it?
   *Intent: forcing function on a flexer.*

## Part 2 — HelloNancy bridge

6. **Pressure to perform**: tennis is lonely. Do people put the same pressure on themselves in relationships and intimacy that athletes do on court?
   *Intent: natural sport→intimacy bridge without jolting.*
7. **Rule-breaking**: Jordans at Wimbledon, asking the crowd where to serve. Why are people still so awkward about talking openly about pleasure?
   *Intent: positions Nick as the right face — unapologetic.*
8. **Showmanship**: you've said "fans are the reason I play — it's all a show." HelloNancy is about making pleasure fun, not serious. How does humor change the way people connect — court, off-court, bedroom?
   *Intent: ties his showman identity directly to brand voice.*
9. **NK Foundation / COVID food deliveries**: you care about people. HelloNancy is about helping people find what works for them. What does it mean to be part of something that's actually trying to help people feel good?
   *Intent: the heart moment. Brand becomes more than a product.*
10. **Time travel**: 14-year-old Nick — choosing between basketball and tennis — sees you doing this campaign today. What does he think?
    *Intent: humanizing closer for the brand section.*

## Part 3 — Why He Hates The Lemon (campaign performance)

11. **The first sighting**: you've smashed rackets, thrown phones, screamed at umpires. But a lemon-shaped vibrator is what finally broke you. Walk us through the moment you first saw it.
    *Intent: sets up the entire premise. He reacts in character.*
12. **The athlete's question**: 230 km/h serve, impossible-angle winners — but you couldn't figure out how to turn it on. How does that make you feel as a professional athlete?
    *Intent: world-class athlete vs. tiny device. Either mock-rage or genuine laugh — both gold.*
13. **The hands**: be honest. Did the lemon make you question your hands? These are the same hands that hit a tweener at Wimbledon, and they couldn't handle a vibrator?
    *Intent: viral-clip line. "Questioning his hands" is the soundbite.*
14. **The journey**: meditation, breathing exercises, calling someone for help at 2am. At what point did you realize the lemon had won?
    *Intent: references storyboard scenes; lets Nick narrate his own defeat in mockumentary tone.*
15. **The closer**: the tagline is "Find the right box." You're Nick Kyrgios — you've found the right serve, the right sneakers, the right tattoo artist. Look into the camera and tell people they need to find the right box.
    *Intent: this is the money shot. Half-serious, half-smirking. Last frame of the campaign.*
