# Rollout — Day-by-Day Seeding Plan

> **Status: DRAFT — to align**

The launch sequence. The point: build mystery before any HelloNancy fingerprint shows up. Cera architecture: question first, brand last.

## The pre-launch arc (5 days before Phase 1 hero film)

### Day -5 — The Plant
**Format**: paparazzi-style creator post (looks organic, not branded)
**Visual**: Nick caught breaking a lemon / smashing a racket
**Caption**: *"is nick OK??"*
**Channel**: a tennis content creator we seed quietly. Not a known brand voice.
**Rule**:
- NO HelloNancy logo, mention, or watermark
- NO branded captions
- Posted from a real creator account that already covers tennis casually
- Filmed phone-vertical, slightly shaky, "is this real?" energy

## Why this order
The CeraVe campaign worked because nobody knew it was an ad until the Super Bowl spot dropped. Days 1–4 here are designed to be undeniably Nick-as-himself, not Nick-as-brand. The brand only appears once we have engagement.

If a viewer Googles "lemon nick kyrgios" on Day -3, the first organic result they should find is a real-looking creator video. Not a press release. Not a HelloNancy account.
